Communication & Transportation

The locals heavily rely on taxis, buses, and bikes. Their main means of communication is the use of telephones,

radios, TV, and newspapers. Guinea's telecommunications networks consist of a single government TV station, a

government-owned radio network, and many private radio stations. Guinea's telephone network isn't adequate, but their cellular networks

have been rapidly increasing. Only a small portion of people have internet access. Conakry has the largest airport in the region, but most

of the flights are used to transport cargo, while most of the railway system have inadequate maintenance.
