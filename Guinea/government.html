Government & Politics

Guinea is an Republican country and the people directly elect both the president and the members

of the legislative body (2). The president may serve a maximum of two five-year terms. The current president

of Guinea is Alpha Condé. The National Assembly of Guinea governs the legislative branch, while the judicial branch is to be

reorganized by the New National Assembly. Over the past few decades, Guinea has improved its foreign relations and belongs to many

international organizations, such as the African Union, World Bank, IMF, ICC, and etc. Guinea's military forces is divided into five branches,

the Republican Guard, National Gendarmerie, Air Force, Navy, and the Army (2).
