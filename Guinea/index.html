Introduction

Guinea is officially known as the Republic of Guinea. It is located in West Africa and is among

the poorest of countries in the region. The nation gained its independence from France on

October 2, 1958 (10). The capital of Guinea is Conakry. The climate is typically tropical and the size of Guinea

spans across a total of 245,857 square kilometers (2). The terrain consist of flat coastal plains to mountainous interior.

There is an estimate of 11,474,383 people populating Guinea (2). Muslims represent 85% of the population, 8% are

Christian, and the other 7% have their own indigenous beliefs. The official spoken language is French.
