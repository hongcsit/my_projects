History

  • 10th & 11th centuries : Ruled by the Ghana Empire.

  • 12th & 13th centuries : Ruled by the Sosso Kingdom.

  • By 1460 : The Songhai Empire came to power until 1582.

  • By 1735 : Guinea became an established Islamic state.

  • 1898 : Guinea was conquered by the French.

  • 19th & 20th centuries : Franch established the boundaries for present day Guinea.

  • 1958 : Gained its independence from the French on October 2, 1958 under the leadership of Sékou Touré.

  • 2008 : December 23, 2008, Guinea's second president, Lansana Conté had died. Shortly after
    Moussa Dadis Camara launched a coup and had dissolved the government.

  • 2009 : December 3, 2009, an assassination attempt at Camara failed. A bullet had grazed his head, but was immediately sent to
    Morocco for surgery the following day. Vice-President Sékouba Konaté served as interim president in Camara's absence.

  • 2010 : June 27, 2010, the first free and fair presidential election were held, but was postponed until November 7th due
    to allegations of electoral fraud.

  • 2010 : November 16, 2010, Alpha Condé emerged victorious as the next president of Guinea, and
    leader of the party, Rally of Guinean People.
