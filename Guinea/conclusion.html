Conclusion

Guinea is a developing country in West Africa. The majority of the population is Muslim and the official language

is French. The region consist of flat coastal plains to hilly and mountainous interior. Guinea has large reserves of bauxite.

Since gaining its independence from the French, the economy has steadily improved. A larger range of people have access to healthcare,

while the Urban areas have an increased number of households with clean water and electricity. Additionally, the completion of the Transguinean

railway network can only further improve the nation's economy and infrastructure for decades to come.
