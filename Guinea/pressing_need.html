Pressing Need

Guinea has poor healthcare, but has greatly improved over the last few decades. However, contracting diseases is still

quite prominent, due to unclean water. Urban areas have inadequate housing and no electricity, which has caused poor

hygiene. Guinea has faced many obstacles, but the most recent is the Ebola outbreak in western Africa. By August 2104, Guinea

had to close its borders to help contain the spread of the virus. This event hindered the country's economic development. Another issue

to the economy is the abandonment of the railway system. Guinea holds an estimate of one-third of the world's reserve of bauxite, but is

unable to transport large loads, due to inefficient transportation.
