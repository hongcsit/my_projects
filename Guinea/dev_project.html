Developmental Projects

One of the projects to help improve the urban areas of Guinea was the Conakry Electricity Network Rehabilitation

and Extension project. The project helped pushed the nation's economy by introducing new jobs and improving the

quality of electricity. This lead to commercial housing construction, development of residential districts, improvement of gas

stations, opening of commercial and medical centers, and establishments of schools. However, it did not meet target goals, due to

high production and operating cost. Further more, the work on bringing electricity to all began in early 2012. Guinea gained enough funds

to start pushing their energy output from 75 megawatts to 122 megawatts, which is a 61% increase in power output. Medical facilities were able

to work overtime to contain the Ebola outbreak. The World Bank, Islamic Development Bank, and African Development bank provided additional

funding to the project to help further rehabilitate and extend the water and electricity network (13). With Guinea having the largest abundance of

water in western Africa, the project should supply roughly 33 urban areas in the country with water by 2015 (13). On average, the people of

Conakry will have an increase of 32% supply of clean water and the hydroelectric capacity should reach about 6000 megawatts (13).
